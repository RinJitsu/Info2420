INFO 2450 - Project 1: Web Site Critique

Trader Joe's - http://www.traderjoes.com/

First Impression: My first impression of the page is it is bright, colorful, and inviting. The main page immediately shows promotions and popular products, and that makes the site feel friendly.

Flow of Information: The site has a top navigation bar for categories such as "Products," "Recipes," and "Find a Store". Images accompanied by text below the navigation are organized and systemized into sections which made it easy to explore.

Site Structure: Organization seems accurate. Everything seems to be grouped together logically. It's pretty easy and clear to find where to go and not get lost in the section.

Graphics/Layout/Multimedia: The graphics are vibrant and fun and contribute to the site without being excessive. Product images at least give the user a general idea of current offerings.

Load Time: The homepage loaded in a couple of seconds. Load time was reasonable and did not interrupt browsing.

Text Readability: Text is readable overall and has good contrast. Background color is calm and doesn't detract from text and reading.

Navigation: The top navigation bar and use of dropdown menus allow for straight forward movement through the site area. Navigation is well executed.

Color Scheme/Look and Feel: The site has one color scheme overall with bright reds, greens, and whites that are consistent with Trader Joe's branding.

Responsive Design: The site responds well should the window be smaller. On mobile devices, the site collapses to a hamburger menu while still maintaining usability.

Unique or Well-Done Features: The site features unique product spotlights and easy access to recipe area. The graphics aligned with Trader Joe's brand and fun, interactive playful graphics allow for an awesome experience!
